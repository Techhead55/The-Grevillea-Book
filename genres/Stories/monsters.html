Monsters
Is it possible to kill the monster inside of you, without killing yourself? If it is, please let me know.
-:-:- 7 attempts.
The number of times I have tried to escape from this world.
20 months.
The time that I have held hostage by a monster called depression.
And over 100’s of scars.
The faded marks left behind by the sharp pieces of metal. Marks that show the battle fought. Marks… that show my story.
Knowing that I may not survive until the morning is haunting, but almost satisfying at the same time. I would take any excuse to escape from this hell hole. This place people called home. But I know that my real home is elsewhere.
Blood trickles down my wrists and legs, making a beautiful painting, made only of red. It’s a familiar sight, only this time the canvas is made up of the sharp rocky surface that lays beneath my bare feet.
I give the area a quick glance over; looking for anyone is may be here to witness my great stunt. But to my luck, there was no one. So I was left to myself, where my scars haunt me and my thoughts kill me. I thought back to just this morning, when my best friends was trying to convince me not to kill myself. It scared me, that I would be leaving all the people I love, because of those monsters. The monsters that ruin my life. Most people in the class don’t know about me. Some don’t even know my name. And some, are the ones that keep me going.
I quickly pulled on my sweater, as I could feel a chilly night set in. I pull a tear-stained note out from the pocket on my sweater, rubbing it between my fingers, before opening it to read it for the very last time.
Re-reading this death letter has created many more tear-stains on the fragile piece of paper. I hold the piece of paper tightly in my hand, hoping that when they recover my body, someone will pass it on to my family. I close my eyes, and take a large step closer towards the edge of cliff. I hear a few stray chunks of the earth slide down the side of this rocky surface, letting me know that the next step I take will be my death step.
I’m about to take my next step, my step to freedom... until I feel an arm wrap around my small waist. A waist that could have only been created by hours of starving and purging. This strange person pulls me away from the cliff edge, letting my bare feet drag along the green grass that sits behind the dead surface.
I open my eyes to come face to face with a blonde haired boy, with blue eyes, and salty tear trails over his pale cheeks.
Unexpectedly, I feel his arms wrapped around my small figure, and naturally my arms lift up to wrap around his waist as well.
We stand on this green meadow for what seems like hours, enjoying this warm cuddle. One that warms us both on this freezing winter’s night. He pulls away from the comforting hug, and leads me towards an old, rusty bench that sits just a few metres form the cliff edge. I hesitate for a moment, but I couldn’t resist the thought of having someone that cares.
We sit there for a moment in silence, because I’m too scared to say something, and I’m sure the boy has too many questions.
“I’m sure so many people care about you. Why would you want to take your life?” The boy questions me. I’m not sure whether or not to reply to this stranger, but I can’t help but feel a sense of care in his voice.
“No one likes me. Except for my two best friends but-“
“Then shouldn’t you try to fight through this, for them. You may not realise it, but not everyone hates you” boy shouted at me. I instantly felt a sense of hate, but this hate changed into something else. One that I can’t exactly put a name on. But whatever it was, it changed me, because I could feel it in the pit of my stomach.
“Why are you here anyway?” It’s not every day that someone takes a stroll near this death trap. I only just found this place a few days ago, hidden amongst thick bushes and a busy high way.
I could see the boy’s face drop when the words left my mouth.
“I’m Emily by the way”. The words burst out of my mouth before I could stop them.
“I’m Noah”.
An awkward silence was created between us for several minutes, until he broke our invisible tension.
“More people care about you than you actually think. I know... because I think the exact same thing.”
He started fiddling around with the zipped on his jumper that blankets him from the cool winds.
And once the green cloth lost contact from his hand, a look of shame spread across his face.
And scars and cuts spread across his wrists. His jumper must have absorbed most of the blood, but small red specks remained on his wounds. His battle wounds.
I pulled my sweater off me as well, and I was just standing there in the cool wind in nothing but my long jeans and a sleeveless shirt.
Once again, we just sit in silence for a while, trying to make sense of what is happening.
He picked up my small hands in his, and just stared at my bare fingernails for several minutes, until he turned over my hands to examine my wrists. Some of the cuts were stilling bleeding; even after 30 minutes, but most of them had dried up, leaving behind specks just like the ones that covered the boy’s cuts.
I could feel tears welling up in my eyes, but I was determined to not let any of them roll down my face. I quickly wiped away a few that were on the verge of falling, and when I looked up again, the boy stared straight into my eyes. And this time, I couldn’t keep in my salty water drops.
I burst into tears, not knowing what else to do.
I quickly pulled my hands out of the boys, and struggled to pull on my sweater. The boy started talking, but I couldn’t bear the thought of being stopped again. I got off the rusty bench and started walking really fast back to where I was originally positioned.
I could hear a pair of feet coming towards me. Ones that I could never out run.
There was only one exit from this boy, from this world. To jump.
I reached the edge of the cliff. I’m about to jump, when I feel the boys hand slide into mine.
I look up at him, and see him give a small nod to me.
“I’m coming with you”.
I could see tears pool in his eyes as well. He held my hand up to his face, and planted a small kiss on the back of my hand.
I held him tight, and let myself love someone one last time. One last time, before we jumped.
